Dámaso Pérez Prado

El Rey del Mambo

Cuban born bandleader, keyboardist, and composer; pioneer of the mambo, foreigneer of modern day Salsa.

Here's a time line of Pérez Prado's life:

  • 1917 - Born in Matanzas, Cuba
  • 1949 - Perez moved to Mexico to form his own band and signed a recording contract with RCA Victor.
  • 1950 - Arranger Sonny Burke heard "Qué rico el mambo" while on vacation in Mexico and recorded it back in the United States as "Mambo Jambo". The single was a hit, which caused Pérez to launch a US tour.
  • 1960 - "Patricia" was featured in the striptease scene in Federico Fellini's 1960 film La Dolce Vita
  • 1987 - His last United States appearance was in Hollywood.
  • 1999 - Posthumously inducted into the International Latin Music Hall of Fame.

“For me, like for all musicians in New York and the world, the only mambo king ever was Pérez Prado.”

Tito Puente
